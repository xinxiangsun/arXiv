Motivation: 银河系中心是暗物质探测的重要目标区域，但现有望远镜如MAGIC的有限视场限制了对其周围扩展区域的详细研究。CTA-LST-1望远镜的宽视场特性使其能够更有效地搜索WIMP湮灭产生的伽马射线线谱。

Method: 使用CTA-LST-1望远镜进行大天顶角观测（天顶角>58度），分析仪器响应函数，开发单望远镜模式下的背景抑制技术，并基于更新的模拟结果进行谱线搜索方法研究。

Result: 研究提供了LST-1在大天顶角观测条件下的系统不确定性分析，改进了伽马射线谱线搜索方法，为20-100 TeV能区的暗物质探测提供了新的技术基础。

Conclusion: CTA-LST-1望远镜的宽视场和大天顶角观测能力使其成为银河系中心暗物质WIMP搜索的有力工具，系统不确定性研究为未来的高能伽马射线天文学观测提供了重要参考。

Abstract: Dark Matter remains a great mystery in modern physics. Among various
candidates, the weakly interacting massive particles (WIMPs) scenario stands
out and is under extensive study. The detection of the hypothetical gamma-ray
emission from WIMP annihilation could act as a direct probe of
electroweak-scale interactions, complementing DM collider searches and other
direct DM detection techniques. At very high energies (VHE), WIMP
self-annihilation is expected to produce gamma rays together with other
Standard Model particles. The galactic center (GC), due to its relative
proximity to the Earth and its high expected DM density, is a prime target for
monoenergetic line searches. IACTs have placed strong constraints on the DM
properties at the GC, with the MAGIC providing the most stringent limits from
20 TeV to 100 TeV, exploiting large zenith angle (LZA) observations. However,
the limited field of view (FoV) of the MAGIC telescopes (< 3.5{\deg} )
prevented a detailed study of the extended region around the GC in which an
enhanced DM density is expected. The LST-1 of the CTAO, located at the Roque de
Los Muchachos Observatory (La Palma, Spain), close to the MAGIC site, has been
observing the GC since 2021. With its wide FoV of 4.5{\deg}, LST-1 could
contribute significantly to the WIMPs search at the GC. The observations are
performed at LZA (ZA > 58{\deg}), which, while required due to the source's low
altitude, also optimizes the detection of gamma rays up to 100 TeV and beyond.
We present a study of the systematic uncertainties in WIMP line emission
searches with LST-1. Our work examines the instrument response functions for
LZA observations, background rejection in monoscopic mode, and includes updated
results from simulations, highlighting new methods for spectral line searches.
